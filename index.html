About Tastybite

Kentucky Fried Chicken (KFC), a globally recognized fast-food chain, was founded by Harland Sanders in the 1930s when he began selling fried chicken from his roadside restaurant in Corbin, Kentucky, during the Great Depression. Sanders developed a unique blend of 11 herbs and spices for his chicken, a recipe that remains a closely guarded secret and is said to be stored in a vault at KFC's headquarters in Louisville, Kentucky. The first KFC franchise opened in Utah in 1952, marking the official start of the company's franchising model, which became a key driver of its rapid expansion.
